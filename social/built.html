Types of Houses

Text

Well over two thirds of the homes in Rockford are single family detached homes--a feature common across the United States. Unlike most urban areas in the US, however, Rockford boasts very few single family attached homes. These are the rowhouses and townhosues common in many other cities.

Most of the homes which are apartments or part of larger structures are rented out and more than half of all rented homes are in these categories.

How Old are the Houses?

 

Almost all of the homes in Rockford were built in the 20th century with many of those still standing being cosntructed sometime in the 80s and 90s. Rockford also boasts as substantial number of pre-war homes--those built before 1939.

Most of the homes built after 2000 are owned by their occupants; most renters live in homes built before 2000.

How many Rooms?

 

Most renters live in homes with between 4 or 5 rooms. A very small percentage of homes in Rockford have less than 4 rooms and most of them are occupied by renters.

How many Bedrooms?

 

Almost three quarters of homes in Rockford have between 2 and 3 bedrooms along with almost three quarters of rented homes having between 2 and 3 bedrooms as well.

Two-to-three bedrooms homes are typical for families with children as are those with larger numbers of bedrooms. Rockford is somewhat unique among cities in having such a large amount of housing units available for families. A sizable portion of the housing stock is suitable for families with 3 or more children though information on the affordability of these homes is unknown in the data available.

Many cities in the country have little housing stock suitable for small to large families but Rockford seems to be an exception. There is housing already built of this type.

How many Vehicles?

 

Around a third of households in Rockford have a single car and about another third have two. Around a quarter have three or more cars and the rest have none.

These facts are not surprising as Rockford has been built for the past decades around automobiles. Single-use zoning has made cars a requirement for commuting and errands.

Rockford is typical for mid-sized cities in the United States regarding car use, that is to say that it is higher than older, larger cities such San Francisco, New York City, Philadelphia, or Boston. These cities have lower car ownership rates and higher use of public transit, bikes, and walking for commutes and errands.
