Deaths from Heart Disease (2020)

 

The term heart disease refers to any disease involving the heart or blood vessels. The most common ones being Coronary Heart Diseases such as angina and heart attacks.

Strokes are also a type of heart disease but are not included in the numbers shown in this graphic. Strokes have been given their own category further down the page.

The CDC estimates that 47% of Americans have something elevating their risk of heart disease including diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and alcohol consumption.[1]

1 in every 4 deaths in the United States is from heart disease.

For more information on heart disease, please visit the CDC page on heart disease.

Deaths from Cancer (2020)

 

Cancers are any of a group of diseases involving abnormal cell growth with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body.

Approximately 22% of cancer deaths can be attributed to tobacco in all of its forms.[2] Many infectious diseases cause cancer and many cases of cancer are due to inherited genetic factors. The five-year survival rate for cancer in the United States is 66%.[3]

Deaths from COVID-19 (2020)

 

COVID-19 is a disease spread by a virus known as Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 or SARS-CoV-2.

The short term affects of COVID-19 vary from person to person but include fever, headache, cough, fatigue, breathing difficulties, and loss of smell/taste. Around 1/3 of people who infected will have no symptoms[4] but most will develop mild to extreme or even critical symtoms ranging from mild pneumonia to respiratry failure.[5]

The long-term effects of COVID-19 infection are currently unknown. Many people experience symptoms long after their infection is over. This is known as Long COVID or post-COVID-19 syndrome. 13.7% of people who tested positive for SARS-CoV-2 had at least one symptom last longer than 12 weeks. [6]

Deaths from Strokes (2020)

 

When brain cells die from a lack of blood, the event is known as a stroke. The two types of stroke are strokes due to lack of blood flow known as ischemic, and strokes due to bleeding known as hemorrhagic.

Stroke risk is increased from many things including tobacco, metabolic syndrome, highblood cholesterol, diabetes, and end-stage kidney disease.
