Deaths from Heart Disease (2020)

 

The term heart disease refers to any disease involving the heart or blood vessels. The most common ones being Coronary Heart Diseases such as angina and heart attacks.

Strokes are also a type of heart disease but are not included in the numbers shown in this graphic. Strokes have been given their own category further down the page.

The CDC estimates that 47% of Americans have something elevating their risk of heart disease including diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and alcohol consumption.[1]

1 in every 4 deaths in the United States is from heart disease.

For more information on heart disease, please visit the CDC page on heart disease.

Deaths from Cancer (2020)

 

Cancers are any of a group of diseases involving abnormal cell growth with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body.

Approximately 22% of cancer deaths can be attributed to tobacco in all of its forms.[2] Many infectious diseases cause cancer and many cases of cancer are due to inherited genetic factors. The five-year survival rate for cancer in the United States is 66%.[3]

Deaths from COVID-19 (2020)

 

COVID-19 is a disease spread by a virus known as Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 or SARS-CoV-2.

The short term affects of COVID-19 vary from person to person but include fever, headache, cough, fatigue, breathing difficulties, and loss of smell/taste. Around 1/3 of people who infected will have no symptoms[4] but most will develop mild to extreme or even critical symtoms ranging from mild pneumonia to respiratry failure.[5]

The long-term effects of COVID-19 infection are currently unknown. Many people experience symptoms long after their infection is over. This is known as Long COVID or post-COVID-19 syndrome. 13.7% of people who tested positive for SARS-CoV-2 had at least one symptom last longer than 12 weeks. [6]

Deaths from Strokes (2020)

 

When brain cells die from a lack of blood, the event is known as a stroke. The two types of stroke are strokes due to lack of blood flow known as ischemic, and strokes due to bleeding known as hemorrhagic.

Stroke risk is increased from many things including tobacco, metabolic syndrome, highblood cholesterol, diabetes, and end-stage kidney disease.

Deaths from Alzheimers (2020)

 

Alzheimer's disease is a neurodegenerative disease that worsens over time. It is the cause of around 2 out of every 3 cases of dementia.

There are no medications or supplements shown to decrease the chances of getting Alzheimers. Death from Alzheimer's occurs as a result of gradual loss of bodily function due to the damaging of brain tissue.

Life expectancy following diagnosis is three to nine years.

Deaths from Diabetes (2020)

 

Type two adult-onset diabetes is caused by sugar.

Sugar in the form of fructose or sucrose is a chronic, dose-dependent liver toxin which is processed in the body as a toxin similar to alcohol. Fructose and Sucrose serve no biological function in the body.

Though it was long believed that saturated fats caused heart disease and diabetes and obesity, this is a disproven mistake.

Deaths from Kidney Disease (2020)

 

Kidney disease or Renal disease includes both diseases of and damage to the kidneys. Diseases can be inflammatory or noninflammatory.

Dialysis and kidney transplants are common treatments though most treatments focus on managing symptoms, controlling progression, or treating co-morbidities that the patient has.

There are many things which increase the chances of kidney disease including diets low in vegetables, lithium medication, lupud, diabetes, and the use of analgesic pain medications.

Deaths from Flu and Pneumonia (2020)

 

Influenza or the flu is an infectious disease caused by influenza viruses. Symptoms in the flu can be mild such as headache, fever, runny nose, muscle pain, coughing, or fatigue; or they could be severe and lead to pneumonia via the virus itself or when a bacteria takes advantage of the body's weakened state.

In a typical year, 5% to 15% of the population contract the flu. Due to the flu being a virus, it mutates rapidly and there are many strains in circulation. Because of this, yearly flu vaccines must be taken. Doctors and medical professions spend much of the year before a flu season predicting which of the present strains will be widespread so that vaccine production can be ramped up in time to prevent its spread.

Occasionally there are particularly lethal strains of the flu such as the Spanish flu in 1918, the Asian flu in 1957, the Hong Kong flu in 1968, the Russian flu in 1977, and the swine flu in 2009.

Pneumonia is a condition in which the lungs become inflammed. Symptoms of pneumonia include coughing mucus, dry coughing, chest pain, fever, and difficulty breathing. Diagnosis can be aided by blood tests or chest x-rays.

The bacteria most associated with pneumonia is Streptococcus pneumoniae as it is typically linked with the flu.
