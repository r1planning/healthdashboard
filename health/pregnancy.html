Rates of Prenatal Care

 

The graph indicates the level of prenatal care using the Kotelchuck Index.

To calculate the level of care for a pregnancy one compared the number of actual prenatal visits to the expected number along with considering when the visits took place (with earlier being considered better).

Level of Care in terms of visits
• Inadequate - less than 50% of expected
• Intermediate - 50% to 79% of expected
• Adequate - 80% to 109% of expected
• Adequate Plus - 110%+ of expected

Low Birth weight Percentage

 

Low birth weight babies weigh less than 5lb 8oz (2500g).

Causes for low birth weight include preterm birth--births in pregancies less than 37 weeks long or an infant being small for its gestation age. There are likely many things which cause low birth weight but prominent amoung them is use of tobacco. Tobacco smoking doubles the chances of an infant being low birth weight.

Air pollutants have also been shown to have a large impact on the chances of low birth weight. General air pollution and particulate matter cause increases in the chances of low birth weight.[11] [12] [13]

Birth Defects and Adverse Pregnancies

 

Adverse Pregnancy outcomes are miscarriages, chromosomal abnormalities, congenital malformations, stillbirths, or births before 34 weeks of gestation.

Many factors influence the rate of adverse pregnancy outcomes. Some which increase the chances are teen pregnancy and pregnancy over 40.

Birth Defects are physical or biochemical abnormalities that are present at birth. Common types include congenital heart disease, down syndrome, cleft lip/palate, spina bifida, club foot, phenylketonuria, and edward's syndome.
